SHARE PURCHASE AGREEMENT — TEST DOCUMENT (HIGH RISK)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This Share Purchase Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of March 1, 2026,
by and between ACME Ventures LLC ("Buyer") and TechCorp Industries Inc. ("Seller").

WHEREAS, Seller owns 100% of the issued shares of DataFlow Solutions Ltd.;
NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the parties agree:


─── ARTICLE 1: PURCHASE AND SALE ───────────────────────────────────────────────

1.1 Sale of Shares
Seller agrees to sell to Buyer all issued and outstanding shares of DataFlow 
Solutions Ltd. for a purchase price of $5,000,000 USD.

1.2 Closing Date
The closing shall occur on April 15, 2026, subject to regulatory approvals.


─── ARTICLE 2: REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES ──────────────────────────────────

2.1 Financial Condition
Seller represents that the financial statements provided are accurate as of 
December 31, 2025. However, Seller makes no representation regarding events 
occurring after that date.

2.2 Material Adverse Change
If a Material Adverse Change occurs, Buyer may terminate this Agreement. A 
"Material Adverse Change" includes any change in market conditions, competitive 
landscape, or general economic downturn.


─── ARTICLE 3: LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION ───────────────────────────────────

3.1 Seller Liability — ⚠️ HIGH RISK
Seller shall be liable for all obligations, debts, liabilities, and claims 
arising from the Company's operations, whether known or unknown, prior to or 
after the Closing Date. This includes any environmental liabilities, regulatory 
violations, intellectual property infringement claims, employee disputes, 
customer lawsuits, vendor obligations, tax liabilities, and any other claims 
of any nature whatsoever.

Seller's liability under this section is UNLIMITED and has NO CAP.

3.2 Buyer Indemnification — ⚠️ HIGH RISK
Buyer shall indemnify Seller against any claims related to post-closing operations, 
but only to the extent such claims exceed $2,000,000 in the aggregate. Below this 
threshold, Buyer assumes no responsibility.

3.3 Indemnification Cap — ⚠️ CRITICAL RISK
Seller's total indemnification obligations under this Agreement shall not exceed 
$250,000 USD. This cap applies to ALL claims, including fraud, willful misconduct, 
and gross negligence.

3.4 Survival Period
All indemnification obligations shall survive for 12 months after Closing, except 
for tax matters which survive for 18 months.

3.5 Basket and Deductible
Buyer must absorb the first $150,000 of any indemnifiable losses before making 
any claim against Seller.


─── ARTICLE 4: TERMINATION RIGHTS ──────────────────────────────────────────────

4.1 Termination for Convenience — ⚠️ HIGH RISK
Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time, for any reason or no reason, 
by providing 15 days' written notice to the other party.

4.2 Change of Control Termination — ⚠️ CRITICAL RISK
The Company's three largest customer contracts (representing 68% of annual revenue) 
contain change-of-control provisions that allow automatic termination upon acquisition. 
Seller has not obtained waivers from these customers.

Customer A (35% of revenue): Auto-terminates on change of ownership
Customer B (22% of revenue): Requires consent, which may be withheld for any reason
Customer C (11% of revenue): May terminate with 30 days notice post-acquisition

4.3 Key Employee Departure
If any of the Company's five key engineers resign within 90 days of Closing, 
Buyer may terminate this Agreement and demand full refund of the purchase price.

4.4 No Cure Period
All termination rights are exercisable immediately without any right to cure. 
Seller has no opportunity to remedy any breach before termination becomes effective.


─── ARTICLE 5: NON-COMPETE AND RESTRICTIVE COVENANTS ───────────────────────────

5.1 Non-Compete — ⚠️ WEAK PROTECTION
Seller agrees not to engage in any business that competes with the Company for a 
period of 9 months following Closing.

Geographic Scope: Within the city limits of San Francisco only
Definition of "Competing Business": Limited to businesses that directly replicate 
the Company's exact product offering as it exists on the Closing Date

5.2 Non-Solicitation — ⚠️ WEAK PROTECTION  
Seller shall not solicit the Company's employees for 6 months after Closing. 
However, this restriction does not apply to:
  - General job postings on LinkedIn or other public platforms
  - Employees who contact Seller proactively
  - Former employees who left the Company before Closing

5.3 Trade Secrets
Seller shall not disclose the Company's confidential information, except that 
Seller may share such information with advisors, investors, or other third parties 
if Seller deems it necessary for legitimate business purposes.

5.4 No Enforcement Mechanism
In the event of a breach of the non-compete provisions, Buyer's sole remedy is 
to seek injunctive relief. Seller shall not be liable for monetary damages arising 
from any competitive activities.


─── ARTICLE 6: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ──────────────────────────────────────────

6.1 IP Assignment — ⚠️ INCOMPLETE
Seller assigns to Buyer all intellectual property developed by Company employees 
during their employment. However, any IP developed by contractors remains the 
property of such contractors unless separate assignment agreements exist.

Seller has not obtained assignment agreements from 7 of the 12 contractors who 
contributed to the Company's core technology platform.

6.2 Founder IP
The Company's co-founder, John Smith, developed the initial version of the software 
while employed at his previous company, MegaTech Corp. No clearance or license has 
been obtained from MegaTech Corp regarding this technology.

6.3 Open Source License Violation
The Company's product incorporates certain GPL-licensed open source components. 
The Company has not released its source code as required by the GPL, and no legal 
review of license compliance has been conducted.


─── ARTICLE 7: REGULATORY AND COMPLIANCE ───────────────────────────────────────

7.1 GDPR Compliance
The Company processes personal data of EU residents but has not appointed a Data 
Protection Officer as required by GDPR. The Company has received 3 data subject access 
requests in the past year, none of which were responded to within the required 30-day 
timeframe.

7.2 SOC 2 Audit
The Company claims to be "SOC 2 compliant" in its marketing materials but has never 
undergone an actual SOC 2 Type II audit. This representation is being actively used 
to win enterprise customers.

7.3 California Consumer Privacy Act
The Company has not implemented a "Do Not Sell My Information" link on its website 
as required by CCPA, despite collecting California resident data.


─── ARTICLE 8: FINANCIAL OBLIGATIONS ──────────────────────────────────────────

8.1 Earn-Out Provision
$2,000,000 of the purchase price is contingent on the Company achieving $8,000,000 
in revenue during the 12 months following Closing. Revenue calculations shall be 
determined by Buyer in its sole discretion.

8.2 Escrow
$500,000 of the purchase price shall be held in escrow for 12 months to secure 
Seller's indemnification obligations. All escrow funds not claimed by Buyer shall 
be released to Seller.

8.3 Payment Terms
The purchase price shall be paid as follows:
  - $1,000,000 at Closing
  - $1,500,000 in 6 equal monthly installments
  - $500,000 in escrow
  - $2,000,000 earn-out (contingent)


─── ARTICLE 9: MISCELLANEOUS ───────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Cayman Islands.

9.2 Dispute Resolution
All disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration in Grand Cayman. 
Each party shall bear its own legal costs regardless of outcome.

9.3 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes 
all prior negotiations, understandings, and agreements, whether written or oral.

9.4 Amendment
This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties.


IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first 
written above.
